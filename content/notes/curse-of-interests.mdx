---
title: "The curse of too many interests."
---

I used to think that being a generalist meant I was fundamentally broken. I would watch my peers dive deep into a single discipline—becoming senior React engineers, or specialized UX researchers—while I kept hitting a wall of boredom and wandering off into another field.

But eventually, I realized that the value isn't in knowing everything about one thing. The value is in knowing the space *between* the things.

When you understand the constraints of the backend database, the realities of the frontend rendering cycle, and the psychological state of the user, you don't just build faster. You build *differently*. You stop trying to solve human problems with code, and you stop trying to solve technical debt with UI.

The curse of too many interests is only a curse if you try to compete with specialists on their own turf. Once you start competing on the intersections, it becomes the ultimate leverage.
