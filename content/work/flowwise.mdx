## Why this mattered

Most personal finance apps are deeply depressing to use. They are essentially historical ledgers—they tell you what you did wrong after you've already done it. 

FlowWise mattered because I wanted to build an app that looked forward, not backward. It needed to be a companion that understood your financial habits and nudged you before you made a bad decision, rather than just showing you a red chart at the end of the month.

## What made it difficult

Finance apps demand absolute precision, but human behavior is entirely irrational.

The technical challenge was building a system robust enough to securely handle SQLite transactions locally on the device (for extreme privacy), while pushing anonymized habit data to Gemini to generate personalized, behavioral nudges. The design challenge was making a financial app feel lightweight and encouraging rather than punitive.

## How I approached it

I started in Figma, not with code. I built a full design system consisting of 40+ components, ensuring the app felt native and fluid.

I focused heavily on micro-interactions. When you categorize an expense, it doesn't just snap into place; it glides. When a nudge arrives, it doesn't look like a warning banner; it looks like a quiet suggestion from a friend. 

Technically, I built it using React Native and Expo, keeping the primary database offline-first. The AI layer acts asynchronously, analyzing spending velocity—for instance, if you are spending your "Dining Out" budget 30% faster than usual, it generates a gentle, context-aware notification before the weekend hits.

## Trade-offs

I sacrificed cloud-sync convenience for local-first privacy. 

Because financial data is so sensitive, I decided early on that the primary database would live on the user's phone. This meant giving up easy multi-device syncing, but it allowed me to guarantee privacy and achieve instantaneous load times. The UI never waits for a network request to render your balance.

## Reflection

FlowWise taught me the power of state transitions. 

In mobile apps, the space *between* screens is just as important as the screens themselves. Motion is context. By spending hours perfecting the gesture navigation and the feeling of entering a transaction, I learned that trust in software isn't just about security—it's about how solid and predictable the interface feels under your thumb.
